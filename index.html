Biography

Biography

Alexandra Hedin is a young Swedish opera singer. She recently graduated from the Academy of Music and Drama in Gothenburg where she completed her Bachelor of Arts in Opera (June 2016).

Alexandra’s previous training has been as a student at the Stockholm Opera Studio, Kulturama, where she sang the parts of Marzelline in Fidelio by Beethoven and Sophie in Der Rosenkavalier by Strauss.

She has prior to Kulturama also taken classes in classical singing at the Falun Conservatory of Music.

It was in 2013 when Alexandra was accepted for the BA course in Opera at the Academy of Arts and Drama in Gothenburg. During her 3 years in training she has been mentored by the opera singer Birgit Louise Frandsen.

For Alexandra’s final year exam in Gothenburg she took on the singing role of Tonina in Antonio Salieri’s Prima la musica e poi le parole, Musical Director and Conductor Henrik Schäfer and Director Mattias Palm.

In the spring of 2016 the opportunity came along for Alexandra to sing the part of Mercedes in Bizet’s Carmen at Åhaga in Borås, a concertante version that was conducted by Joachim Gustafsson.

During her opera training Alexandra has had the experience of singing in several productions and has taken on roles such as Nedda in an extract from Pagliacci by Leoncavallo and as Captain Lovelock in the opera by the same name by J Duke. She has also participated in many master classes lead by, amongst others, Barbara Bonney, Nadia Kurem, Barbro Marklund, Gerhard Markson and Luciana Serra.

Throughout her training and career Alexandra has received a number of scholarships. She has been rewarded by the Royal Swedish Academy of Music, Tobison’s Foundation, the Adlerbertska Foundation, Lasse Lönndahl’s Travel Scholarship (a foundation for music and opera talent) and Markussens Foundation.
